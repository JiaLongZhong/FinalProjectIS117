DotCom

  • The video starts off by talking about how the internet has forever changed the world.
  • The video then goes on to talk about Amazon and eBay and how those companies were creating an ecommerce website for selling things online.
  • These websites were blowing up in popularity and the creators were making lots of money every day.
  • People on wall street were surprised how fast the internet was growing.
  • The internet was a network growing like we’ve never seen before.
  • In just 4 years when it went public it had 50 million users.
  • Moore's Law is a law that says that transistors double every 18 months.
  • Another law is Metcalfe’s law. This law says every user that comes onto the network does not increase the networks value by +1 but much more.
  • Metcalfe’s Law caused exponential growth in the number of users on the internet.
  • Breakthrough technologies usually cause bubbles.
  • Amazon was controversial to investors because some people did not think a bookstore can be profitable.
  • Websites like Amazon also had to get customers to trust them with their credit card info.
  • Public key cryptography allowed for safe credit card info transfer.
  • The private key is used to decrypt the data.
  • eBay IP0’d and they were worth $2B as they opened.
  • Investors saw the success of eBay and Amazon and it made them invest into any dot com stock they can get their hands on.
  • With companies like eBay and Amazon, many programmers moved to California in hopes of making their own companies or to work with the biggest.
  • The success of tech companies brought about large waves of day traders and resulted in companies and people throwing their money into anything tech.
  • Even though many tech companies were fundamentally unsound, many invested in them because of the fear of missing out on massive return.
  • Telecommunication companies were laying fiber optic cables all over the country at an unsustainable rate without any concern for money or immediate returns.
  • The Fed did nothing about the explosion in tech only until it was too late. They raised interest rates before the bubble burst, but it was not enough.
  • The stock market plummeted 25% and with the terrorist attacks of 9/11, America was done inflating.
  • Dot com boom was helpful for creating the internet infrastructure we have today and creating more stronger companies.
