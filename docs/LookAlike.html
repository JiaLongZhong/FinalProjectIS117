Look Alike Analysis:

  • Start with an Advertiser running a campaign on his/her products or services.
  • Need to identify super users from the normal user.
  • Super user are potential loyal customers.
  • Normal user does less transaction.
  • Get a huge number of computer IDs/ online fingerprinting.
  • Create a feature vector: A variable/behavior that separates super user and normal user.
  • Basically, setting behaviors into a positive or negative group.
  • Look Alike Models: act as a tree, as two new branches are created for each vector.
  • They are basically grouping customer base on behaviors.
  • For instance, people who watch football and are male are more likely to buy your product than females who watch football.
  • They can get information on super user preference, such as which device they tend to use to buy the product.
  • They use the data to compare people's behavior with others.
  • They can find variables that prevent you from buying the product.
  • The whole model is basically used to maximize the amount of money a product/services can produce.
